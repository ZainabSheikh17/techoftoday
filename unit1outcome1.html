1.7 GDPR Gudielines

General data protection regulation (GDPR) is a privacy law that was put into action in May 2018. Its purpose is to ensure personal data of individuals is protected when processed by external sources. Since that law was introduced, it has largely impacted digital projects and platforms that process data.

For example, in recruitment the majority of the companies now take online CV applications as GDPR requires employers to hold responsibility for protecting data of the candidate, as it includes these include name, address and contact details. According to GDPR this makes the employers the “Data Controllers” and candidates the “Data Subjects”.

If software technology is not used correctly (following the GDPR laws), data breach can occur, which can negatively impact data in project or any other data online. For example in 2016, a massive data breach took place in 2016 when a third party exposed personal data of more than thousand healthcare workers. This did not only break the GDPR law, but potentially allow hackers to intervene with NHS patients data, as they would be able to access the data. Therefore it is really important to protect information using software technology in today's society.
